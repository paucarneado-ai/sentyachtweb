Submit valuation request

Before making any decision about selling, you need to know where your boat sits in the market. We provide an indicative price range based on recent transactions, overall condition and current market dynamics. This is not an official appraisal — it is a well-founded professional opinion that helps you decide with real information.

Why request a valuation?

  • Understand where your boat stands against what is actually selling in the market right now.
  • Avoid a price too high that drives buyers away, or too low that costs you money.
  • Decide whether now is the right time to sell, or whether waiting makes more sense.
  • Get an independent professional view — at no cost and with no obligation to proceed.

What factors do we consider?

Make, model and type

Some builders and models hold their value better. The type of vessel defines the market segment.

Year of build

Age matters, but doesn't tell the whole story. A well-maintained older boat can outperform a newer one in poor shape.

Length and capacity

Size, cabins and capacity define the buyer profile and the expected price range.

Condition

Hull, engines, electronics, upholstery, systems. Real condition determines the gap between listing price and closing price.

Maintenance history

Documented services, recent refits and commissioning add tangible value and build buyer confidence.

Location and market

The marina, the geographic area and current market conditions all influence demand and the type of buyer available.
